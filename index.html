UX CASE STUDY fOR MOBILE APP DESIGN CHALLENGE:

Eatspire is an app designed to provide the most efficient and inspiring in-store grocery shopping experience for people on the go. Through our research, we discovered that modern users view traditional in-store grocery shopping as an enjoyable activity. Online grocery shopping provides speed and convenience but takes away from the experience. Hence, our goal was to create an app that enhances the in-store shopping experience, inspiring our users to buy new products and try new recipes at home, while still providing the convenience of checking out online.

TIMELINE:

3-weeks.

MY ROLE:

All team members worked together on all parts of the project. For the final solution, I was responsible for prototyping along with another team member.

RESEARCH OBJECTIVES:

• How does in-store shopping differ from online shopping, and why do our potential users prioritize one or the other?

• How do in-store grocery shoppers go through the shopping process? What factors affect their decision?

• If in-store grocery shoppers were to use our app, what would he/she benefit from?

• What would the shoppers think when using the app during in-store shopping?

• How do we stand out from other similar apps, what is the pain-point of others?
